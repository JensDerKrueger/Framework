Task 1: Patch Creation

The solution iterates over all scene objects, requests their tessellated triangle mesh, and turns every triangle into one radiosity patch. Each patch stores its vertices, center, normal, area, diffuse reflectance, emission, and initial radiosity.

Task 2: Form-Factor Estimate

The lecture form factor is an area integral. The exercise uses one center sample per patch instead. This replaces the two area integrals by the emitter area Aj, the center distance, the two cosine terms, and a visibility term computed separately.

Task 3: Visibility

Visibility is approximated with one center-to-center ray. The ray starts slightly away from the first patch, and the two patches are considered visible if no intersection lies before the target center.
