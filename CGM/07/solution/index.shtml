Shadow-Ray Transmittance

The shadow ray starts at the current primary-ray sample and marches toward the light. Its length is clamped to the volume boundary with distanceToVolumeExit. At each shadow sample, the shader applies the transfer function and uses the resulting alpha as the local density for shadowing.

Lighting Estimate

The accumulated optical depth is converted into a transmittance with an exponential. A value near one means that most light reaches the sample; a value near zero means that the volume between the sample and the light absorbs most of the light. Very transparent primary samples skip the shadow ray and receive full direct light.

Primary Ray

The primary ray still performs front-to-back emission-absorption raycasting. The new step is the lighting multiplication before opacity correction and compositing. This keeps the transfer function responsible for visible opacity while the shadow ray determines how much direct light reaches the sample.
