AO Pass Setup

The first SSAO pass is an additional image-space pass after G-buffer creation. It renders into rawAmbientOcclusionBuffer. The shader only needs the position and normal buffers, because it estimates local occlusion from nearby screen-space geometry.

SSAO Shader

The shader builds a local hemisphere around the current normal. It places more samples near the visible point than far away, projects the sample positions back into the G-buffer, and counts how many sample directions are blocked by closer geometry.

Smoothing Pass

The raw SSAO estimate is noisy because only a limited number of hemisphere samples are used per pixel. The solution therefore writes the raw result first and then smooths it with two full-screen passes. The blur is separable: first horizontal, then vertical. This gives a 5 x 5-like smoothing effect with 10 samples instead of 25. The blur is also depth-aware: nearby pixels with similar view-space depth are averaged strongly, while pixels across depth discontinuities have little influence.

Deferred Lighting

The final shader reads the AO texture and uses it to reduce ambient light in locally hidden regions. The solution also applies it as a contact term for direct light to make the effect easier to see in this compact example.
