Recursion and Ray Handling

The assignment extends the previous ray caster with secondary rays. Therefore traceRay first needs a termination condition: when recursion depth reaches 0, the background color is returned. If the current ray misses the scene, the background color is returned as well.

Reflection and Refraction

For reflective materials, the solution creates a reflected secondary ray. Its origin is shifted slightly along the surface normal so numerical imprecision does not make the new ray immediately hit the same surface again. 

Loading Scene.cpp...

For refractive materials, Vec3::refract computes a transmitted direction. If refraction is possible, the sign of the dot product between the refracted direction and the normal indicates whether the ray leaves or enters the material. The recursive call then uses the appropriate index of refraction.

Local Lighting and Composition

The local Phong lighting from the starter code is largely preserved. It still computes ambient, diffuse, and specular terms and uses shadow rays to decide whether direct light reaches the hit point. 

Loading Scene.cpp...

The final color is the weighted sum from the assignment: local lighting, reflected light, and transmitted light. Purely local materials use only the local term. Reflective materials use Schlick reflectivity as the reflection weight. For refractive materials, the material's local part is applied first; the remaining part is split between reflection and transmission.

Schlick Reflectivity

Material::getReflectivity implements the Schlick approximation used in the assignment. For refractive materials, R0 is computed from the index of refraction. For reflective but non-refractive materials, the simplified exercise model uses 1 - local as the base reflectivity.
