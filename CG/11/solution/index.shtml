Exercise 1: Sphere Texture Coordinates

The sphere first computes the normal at the hit point. For textured spheres, this normal is used as the radial direction from the sphere center. That direction is rotated with the Euler angles described in the assignment and then converted to polar coordinates.

The angle from atan2 provides u, and the angle from acos provides v. Both values are normalized to [0, 1]. They are then scaled by the sphere's texture parameters and shifted by the bias.

Exercise 2: Procedural Checkerboard Texture

The checkerboard texture alternates between black and white texels in both directions. The first texel is black, each column inverts the current value, and each new row starts with the opposite value from the previous row. This creates the checkerboard pattern required by the assignment.

Exercise 3: Border Modes

Texture coordinates may lie outside the image. The border mode defines how such coordinates are mapped back to texels. REPEAT continues the texture periodically, CLAMP_TO_EDGE clamps to the nearest valid texel, and MIRRORED_REPEAT repeats the texture while mirroring every second repetition.

Exercise 4: Texture Filtering

Nearest-neighbor filtering chooses the texel closest to the continuous texture coordinate. Bilinear filtering reads the four surrounding texels and interpolates first along u and then along v.
