Exercise 1: Octree Construction

The slab-based ray-box test already exists in the exercise code. The AABB function needed for construction is overlaps: it checks whether a primitive's bounding box touches a child cell. The builder leaves scene objects in their original vector and stores only indices in octreeObjectIndices. The root bounds are expanded to a cube. Each inner node splits its cube into eight children. Small primitives are inserted into all overlapping children, while objects that overlap many children remain in the current node.

Exercise 2: Octree Traversal

Traversal uses the root AABB only to compute the entry and exit interval. Inside an inner node, the eight child cells are traversed with a 3D DDA. The traversal visits only cells that the ray actually crosses, from front to back. maxT is reduced as soon as a nearer intersection is found. A mailbox stamp prevents duplicated primitives from being tested more than once per ray.
