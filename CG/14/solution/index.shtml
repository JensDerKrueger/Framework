Triangle Rasterization

The triangle rasterizer first restricts the work to the triangle's bounding box. It then evaluates the edge functions at the first pixel center and updates them incrementally while scanning the box. The normalized edge values become barycentric interpolation weights. Because the cube is projected with perspective, color and depth are interpolated with the projected vertices' inverseZ values. This avoids affine screen-space interpolation artifacts that would otherwise be visible on the rotating cube.
