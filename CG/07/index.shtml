Sheet 7: Ray Casting & Shadows

Exercise: Ray casting (10 points)

The provided classes form the basic structure of the implementation. Familiarize yourself with that structure and pay attention to TODO markers that indicate missing code.

Implement Raytracer.render in the Raytracer class so that the given image is filled with color values and the scene becomes visible.

Use Raytracer.computeRay to compute a ray from the origin through the desired screen coordinates. Use Raytracer::traceRay to trace that ray through the scene and compute its color.

Your implementation must support supersampling. Use a regular distribution of samples within each pixel. The number of samples in x- and y-direction is stored as a member variable of Raytracer.

Exercise: Rendering spheres and shadows (10 points)

Implement Sphere so the colored spheres become visible. Then extend the ray tracer with shadows by completing the missing parts of Scene.traceRay.

Shadows are computed by shooting an additional shadow ray from the hit point toward each light source. Your implementation must support multiple point lights.

A key detail is avoiding repeated self-intersections caused by limited floating-point precision. To avoid numerical artifacts at the hit point, offset the computed point by a small epsilon along the normal. This tolerance should be scaled sensibly for the scene.
